Miso Ginger Turkey with Rice

Prep Time: 10 minutes Cook Time: 25 minutes Servings: Servings: calories: 483

Ingredients

FOR THE MEAT MIXTURE:

2 lbs (908 g) ground turkey 93/7

12 oz (340 g) broccoli slaw

½ medium (100 g) sweet onion

1 tbsp (15 ml) olive oil

1 tbsp (15 ml) soy sauce

2 tsp (2 g) sesame seeds optional

FOR THE MISO GINGER DRESSING:

1 cup (100 g) carrots shredded

1½ tbsp (22 ml) olive oil

1 tsp (5 ml) sesame oil

2½ tbsp (37 ml) rice wine vinegar

1½ tbsp (30 g) white miso paste

2 tsp (10 g) ginger minced

2 tbsp (30 ml) water

FOR THE RICE:

2½ cups (375 g) cooked rice

Directions

FOR THE RICE:

Cook enough rice to have 2½ cups of cooked rice. 1 cup of dry rice will yield about 3 cups of cooked rice depending on what kind you use.

FOR THE MEAT MIXTURE:

Heat ½ tbsp of oil in a large skillet over medium high heat.

Add the turkey and cook until it has browned. Boil off any water if there is any remaining in the skillet. Lightly season with salt and pepper.

Heat another skillet over medium high heat and add ½ tbsp of oil.

Dice the onion add to the skillet. Cook for 2-3 minutes and then add in the broccoli slaw.

Cook until the slaw has softened. You may find it helpful to add some water to the skillet to create steam and cook the vegetables faster.

Once both the turkey and vegetables have finished cooking, combine the two into one mixture and add 1 tbsp of soy sauce. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

FOR THE MISO GINGER DRESSING:

Add all of the ingredients to a blender. Blend until you reach your desired consistency. Longer for smoother, shorter for a coarse dressing. I prefer to shoot for somewhere in the middle.

PLATING

This recipe makes 5 servings. Divide your ingredients evenly between your 5 containers. Top each with sesame seeds if you desire.
