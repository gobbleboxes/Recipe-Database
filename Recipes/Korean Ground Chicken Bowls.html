Korean Ground Chicken Bowls

Prep Time: 10 minutes Cook Time: 30 minutes Servings: Servings: calories: 480

Ingredients

FOR THE CHICKEN AND SAUCE

2 lbs (908 g) ground chicken (97/3) ground turkey will also work

1 tbsp (15 g) oil

3 tbsp (45 g) maple syrup

3 tbsp (45 g) gochujang

1 tsp (10 g) sesame oil

2 tbsp (30 g) soy sauce

2 tbsp (30 g) chicken stock or broth

2 tsp (10 g) minced garlic

FOR THE RICE:

2½ cups (375 g) cooked rice

FOR THE STIR FRY VEGETABLES

1½ cups (150 g) matchstick carrots

1 medium (200 g) onion

1 tbsp (15 g) minced ginger

1 medium (150 g) green pepper

12 oz (340 g) broccoli fresh is preferred for this recipe

1 tbsp (15 g) oil

Directions

FOR THE RICE:

Cook enough rice to have 2½ cups of cooked rice. 1 cup of dry rice will make around 2-3 cups of cooked rice depending on what kind you use.

FOR THE CHICKEN AND SAUCE

Cook the chicken in a large skillet over medium high heat with a bit of oil. Give it a chance to brown before you break it up and mix/flip it. Season lightly with salt and pepper.

While the chicken is cooking you can prepare your sauce and cut your vegetables. Mix the soy sauce, gochujang, sesame oil, chicken stock, garlic, and maple syrup.

Once the chicken has finished cooking, pour in the sauce and stir to coat.

FOR THE STIR FRY VEGETABLES

While the chicken is cooking, prepare all of your vegetables. Cut your onion into thin slices, the broccoli into small florets, green pepper into a large dice and the ginger into a mince. If you don't buy the pre cut carrots you will need to cut them into matchsticks as well.

Heat a large pot over medium high heat and add a bit of oil. Add in the onion, peppers, and ginger and allow to develop color. After a couple of minutes add in the carrots and broccoli. It may help speed up the cooking process to add a splash of water and cover the pot to steam the carrots and broccoli. Season lightly with salt and pepper.

Once the vegetables have finished, add in the chicken and stir to combine.

PLATING

This recipe makes 5 servings. Add ½ cup of rice to each dish and divide the chicken mixture evenly.
