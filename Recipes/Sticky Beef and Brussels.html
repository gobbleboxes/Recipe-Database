Sticky Beef and Brussels

Prep Time: 10 minutes Cook Time: 20 minutes Servings: Servings: calories: 592

Ingredients

2 lbs (908 g) ground beef 85/15

1 lb (454 g) Brussels sprouts frozen

1¼ cup (125 g) shredded carrots you can buy these preshredded or do it yourself

1 tbsp (15 g) oil

1 tbsp (15 g) ginger grated

1 tbsp (15 g) garlic grated

4½ cups (675 g) cooked rice

FOR THE SAUCE:

¼ cup (84 g) honey

2 tbsp (30 g) soy sauce

5 tbsp (75 g) chicken stock

1 tsp (5 g) sesame oil

2 tsp (10 g) rice vinegar

Directions

FOR THE RICE:

Cook enough rice to yield 4½ cups of cooked rice. 1 cup of dry rice will make about 2-3 cups of cooked rice depending on what kind you use.

FOR THE BEEF AND BRUSSELS

Add a bit of oil to a skillet over medium high heat. Add the beef and cook until it has browned. Season lightly with salt and then set aside. NOTE: For proper browning, don't break up the meat immediately when you put it in the skillet. Give it some time to develop color.

While the beef is cooking, microwave your frozen Brussels sprouts according to the package. Cut the Brussels sprouts into quarters.

Using a vegetable peeler, shred your carrots into thin strips. Feel free to buy pre-shredded carrots.

In a different skillet, add some oil over medium high heat and place the Brussels sprouts in the pan cut side down so they can develop color. After 3-5 minutes, create some room in the center and add the ginger and garlic and more oil if needed. Cook for 1-2 minutes.

Add in the carrots and cook 2-3 minutes or until soft.

FOR THE SAUCE:

Mix together the honey, soy sauce, stock, vinegar, and sesame oil until combined.

Add the beef and sauce into the skillet with the vegetables and allow the sauce to thicken with the heat. Stir and season to taste.

PLATING

This recipe makes 6 servings. Divide your ingredients evenly between your 6 containers.
