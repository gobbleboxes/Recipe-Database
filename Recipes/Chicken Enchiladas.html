Chicken Enchiladas

Prep Time: 1 hour Cook Time: 30 minutes

Ingredients

FOR THE ENCHILADAS:

2 ½ lbs (1135 g) boneless skinless chicken breast

28 oz (794 g) can fire roasted tomatoes

1.33 tbsp (20 ml) white vinegar

¼ cup (40 g) red onion diced

2 Tbsp (30 g) garlic minced

1.33 Tbsp (20 ml) ancho chili powder

2 tsp (10 g) sugar

1 tsp (1 g) oregano

1 tsp (3 g) salt

2 tsp (10 ml) olive oil

10 (360 g) low carb tortillas

5 Tbsp (37 g) queso fresco

1 cup (112 g) Mexican shredded cheese

FOR THE PICO DE GALLO:

5 (300 g) Roma tomatoes

½ medium (100 g) red onion

¼ cup (1 g) cilantro chopped

1 Tbsp (15 ml) lime juice

1 (20 g) jalapeño pepper

Salt and pepper to taste

Directions

FOR THE CHICKEN:

Add the chicken and 1 cup of water to a crock pot and cook on high for 3 hours or until cooked.

After the chicken has completed cooking, shred it using two forks or a hand mixer.

FOR THE ENCHILADAS:

Dice a red onion until you have ¼ of a cup. Heat 2 tsp of olive oil in a skillet over medium high heat and cook the onion and 2 Tbsp minced garlic until the onion is soft. Be sure to stir frequently to ensure the garlic doesn’t burn.

Add the onion, garlic, 2 cans of tomatoes, 1 ⅓ Tbsp vinegar, 2 tsp sugar, 1 ⅓ Tbsp chili powder, 1 tsp oregano, and 1 tsp salt to a blender. Blend until smooth.

Pour half of the mixture into the skillet over medium low heat and cook for 10-12 minutes.

Once all the chicken is shredded, add it to the pan with ½ the sauce and mix to incorporate.

Divide the meat mixture into 10 equal parts and put one portion into each tortilla along with a sprinkling of shredded Mexican cheese.

Roll the tortilla up and place it seam side down in a 13”x9” pan. Sprinkle the remaining cheese over the top of the enchiladas as well as the last half of the red sauce.

Bake the enchiladas for 10 minutes at 350°F.

FOR THE PICO DE GALLO:

Cut the tomatoes in half the long way and scoop out the seeds. Dice the tomatoes and place in a large bowl.

Dice ½ of a red onion and add to the the tomatoes.

Cut the stem off of the jalapeño pepper. Cut the pepper into small pieces and add to the mixture.

NOTE: If you aren’t a fan of heat, then take out the ribs before chopping up the peppers.

Cut the stems off of a small bunch of cilantro and finely chop the leaves until you have ¼ cup, add to the bowl.

Cut a lime in half and squeeze its juice into the mixing bowl. Stir all of the ingredients together and add salt and pepper to taste.

PLATING

This recipe makes 5 servings. To each of your containers add 2 enchiladas and divide the Pico de Gallo evenly 5 ways. You may want to store this in a smaller separate container so you don’t have to reheat it in the microwave.
